California SWAP 2025 Explorer work in progress

An interactive map and AI assistant for exploring California's State Wildlife Action Plan (SWAP) 2025 — its conservation provinces and units, Species of Greatest Conservation Need, and the strategies and targets that guide their recovery.

← Open the App

About this project

An interactive interface to the California Department of Fish and Wildlife's State Wildlife Action Plan 2025, built by the Boettiger Lab in the Department of Environmental Science, Policy, and Management at UC Berkeley.

State Wildlife Action Plans are the conservation blueprints every U.S. state maintains to keep species from becoming endangered. California's 2025 revision organizes the state into a nested geography of eight provinces and 46 conservation units, identifies the Species of Greatest Conservation Need (SGCN) in each, and lays out the conservation targets and strategies intended to sustain them. This app lets you browse those layers on a live map and ask natural-language questions that translate into SQL queries over the underlying data.

This is a work in progress. Layers, models, and capabilities are being added and refined.

How it works

The map renders open geospatial datasets directly from cloud-native formats (PMTiles vector tiles and GeoParquet) hosted on the National Research Platform. No tile server is involved — your browser fetches only the bytes it needs to render the current view.

The AI assistant translates plain-English questions into SQL using DuckDB over the same Parquet files, with column schemas and dataset descriptions discovered automatically through a SpatioTemporal Asset Catalog (STAC). This is how it can answer questions about the SGCN species list, conservation targets, and strategies — planning tables that have no map geometry of their own.

Low-carbon, open models. The assistant is powered by open-weight large language models (GLM, Qwen3, GPT-OSS 120B, Kimi K2, MiniMax) running on the NRP open-llm-proxy, a shared university inference service running on hardware powered substantially by renewable energy. Compared to large commercial inference services, this approach reduces both the energy and the data-privacy footprint of every conversation. Carbon and energy use are tracked on our companion sustainability dashboard.

The full agent stack — map, chat, MCP tool server, and STAC catalog — is open-source. See the project repository and the geo-agent core library.

Working with the Data Assistant

If you wish the app could do something — just ask the assistant. Things people often reach for a settings menu to do (adjust transparency, export a CSV, filter a layer, run a cross-dataset query) the assistant can already do in plain language. If it can't, it will say so and usually suggest the closest thing it can.

A few of the actions people most often ask for:

  • Adjust layer styling and transparency: "Make the SGCN ranges 40% transparent", "Color the conservation units by type", "Outline the provinces in black."
  • Show, hide, or filter layers: "Turn on the salmonid ESU/DPS units and hide the provinces", "Show only bird ranges", "Filter the ESU/DPS layer to habitat that is anthropogenically blocked."
  • Zoom and pan: "Zoom to the South Coast province", "Center the map on the Bay-Delta."
  • Query the planning tables: "Which taxonomic group has the most SGCN species?", "List the conservation strategies for the Central Valley and Sierra Nevada province."
  • Combine layers in an analysis: "How many SGCN species have ranges overlapping the Deserts province?" — the assistant writes the spatial SQL and shows the result.

And a few interface tips that aren't visually obvious:

  • The Data Assistant panel is resizable — drag its left edge to give chat more or less width.
  • Use the header button to minimize the panel; a floating "show" button restores it.
  • On phones and tablets, the panel auto-collapses to overlay mode so the full map stays visible.
  • PMTiles layers show a legend for their color categories. Hover over any feature for a tooltip with that feature's live attributes — names, categories, IDs — direct from the source data.
  • Every tool call the assistant runs is logged in the per-turn timeline — with timings — so you can see what it queried, with what arguments, and what came back.

Data layers & sources

Every layer draws from the California SWAP 2025 release, published by CDFW under a CC-BY-4.0 license and reprocessed into cloud-native formats by the Boettiger Lab. Click "STAC" beside any entry for the full machine-readable metadata — schemas, column descriptions, license, lineage, and download URLs. Entries marked SQL are tabular planning data the assistant can query but that are not drawn as map layers.

SWAP Regions

Provinces 8 features
The eight statewide analysis regions of SWAP 2025. The 2025 revision adds an Anadromous province relative to the seven regions in SWAP 2015. ProvinceID is the join key to the conservation units.
STACSource
Conservation Units 46 features
Polygons subdividing the provinces into terrestrial ecoregions and freshwater, marine, and anadromous units. Each unit references its parent province via ParentProvinceID, and the targets and strategies tables reference units by name.
STACSource
Bay-Delta Conservation Unit 1 feature
The single Sacramento–San Joaquin Bay-Delta unit polygon from the SWAP 2025 reference geographies.
STACSource

Species of Greatest Conservation Need

SGCN Species Ranges (v4) 422 ranges
Range polygons for the SGCN vertebrates — amphibians, birds, fish, mammals, and reptiles. Each range links to the SGCN species list via ParentSpeciesKey, and traces to a source CDFW BIOS dataset.
STACSource
SGCN Species List 1,437 species SQL
The full list of Species of Greatest Conservation Need in SWAP 2025, spanning all seven taxonomic groups — amphibians, birds, fish, invertebrates, mammals, plants, and reptiles (only the five vertebrate groups have mapped ranges). Each row carries the species' listing status, SGCN status, and a climate-vulnerability indicator. SpeciesKey is the join key to the range polygons and the species × unit crosswalk.
STACSource
SGCN × Conservation Unit 4,268 rows SQL
Crosswalk recording which conservation units each SGCN species occurs in — the basis for "which species are in this unit / which units hold this species" questions. ParentProvSpeciesKey joins to the species list's SpeciesKey; ConUnit names the conservation unit.
STACSource

Marine & Anadromous

Salmonid ESU / DPS (NOAA) 37 features
NOAA Evolutionarily Significant Units and Distinct Population Segments for anadromous salmonids, classified by habitat accessibility (accessible, naturally blocked, anthropogenically blocked, likely extirpated) and ESA listing status.
STACSource
Marine Bioregions 3 features
Three polygons partitioning California's marine waters, from the SWAP 2025 marine reference layers.
STACSource

Conservation Planning Tables

Conservation Targets 460 targets SQL
The habitats and species systems SWAP 2025 identifies for conservation action, one set per conservation unit and typed as Terrestrial, Freshwater Aquatic, Marine, or Anadromous Species. Joins to the conservation units by name (ConsUnit); each target's TargetID is referenced by the strategies table.
STACSource
Conservation Strategies 525 actions SQL
The conservation actions SWAP 2025 proposes to sustain its targets — each tied to the threat it addresses and grouped into strategy categories (Direct Management, Partner Engagement, Data Collection and Analysis, Law and Policy, Land Use Planning, Environmental Review, Outreach and Education, Management Planning). Joins to the targets via ParentTargetID → TargetID.
STACSource
